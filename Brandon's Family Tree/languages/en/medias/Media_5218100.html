Glennadine Ketterman Auvil Obituary

Glennadine Ketterman Auvil, age 84 years, a resident of Elkins WV, departed this life Thursday morning, March 2, 2017 at the Good Samaritan Hospital in Cincinnati, OH after a brief illness.
She was born, Monday, May 30, 1932 at Elkins WV, a daughter of Glenn Whittier and Jessie J. Shreve Ketterman. Her husband of 59 years, Dallas Auvil Jr. of Belington preceded her in death. Surviving are one daughter, Susan Ellen Auvil-Novak (Ron) of Westlake OH; and two sons, Glenn Craig Auvil (Mary) of Simpsonville, SC; and Dallas Gregory Auvil (Melissa) of Cincinnati, OH: two brothers, Randall Ketterman of MA, her twin brother Glenn Whittier Ketterman Jr. (Faye) of Elkins; two sisters, Mary Davis of FL, Patricia "Pat" Nestor of Elkins; seven grandchildren, Daniel, Betsy, Joy, Andy, Emily Sam, and Nick; two great grandchildren, John and Lilly: and several nieces and nephews.
Preceding her in death are one brother, James Franklin "Bus" Ketterman; and three sisters, Ruth Ketterman, Jean Cloninger and Joann Buchanan.
Glennadine graduated from Homestead High School in Florida with the class of 1950 and married Dallas on August 16, 1953. A devoted mother and grandmother she worked outside the home to help support her family. Glennadine had a passion for animals and flowers, enjoyed needlework and was an avid reader.
She also had a heart for women and children and for their benefit she built a women's center in Kabingo, Uganda that will be named in her honor.
A graveside service will be conducted on Saturday, March 11, 2017 at 11 AM at the Maplewood Cemetery at Elkins, WV. The Reverend Dr. Basil A. Hensley will officiate and interment will follow. The family suggests that expressions of sympathy be in the form of contributions to Hope for Kabingo, P. O. Box 54744, Cincinnati, OH 45254 or West Virginia Caring, 169 Diamond Street, Elkins, WV 26241 or the Randolph County Humane Society, P. O. Box 785, Elkins, WV 26241.
