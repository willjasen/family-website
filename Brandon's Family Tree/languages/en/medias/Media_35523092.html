Notes
This picture has been colorized.
Monnie Kerns is the woman on the far right according to Peggy and is her maternal grandmother.
The girl Callie (pronouned cal-ee) is in front of the man on the left.
Woman on the left holding the baby is Peggy's great grandmother. She was the mother to all of these younger people. Peggy says that she was told that she was a McCoy before she married. She was married to the man to her right (James Lane). Monnie's last name was Lane. That makes the great grandmother Mary Jane Lane.
Joseph Lane is the oldest man to the right of James Lane and is James' father. Joseph was an immigrant from Ireland and came over on a ship. The boat that came here docked in Savannah, GA.
The woman to his right is named "Little Dove" and was a Shawnee Indian, she is his wife.
The baby on the left gave this picture in 5 pieces to a woman who restored it and put it back together.
Peggy says that she remembers aunt Callie and saw her lots. Callie was at Indian Path with illness and didn't live much past that. Callie lived in Fort Blackmore. Peggy remembers spending a weekend with her up on a hill and she had a bunch of chickens. They didn't put the chickens up in a lot so they had to watch where they stepped.
Peggy says that this picture was discovered about a year after Velva passed away.
The small girl on the right (Bessy Lane) was raped and murdered at around the age of 15.
Now that I have added all siblings of Monnie's into the tree, I suspect that 5 of her 6 siblings are in this photo, which would place this photo having been taken somewhere between 1902 and 1906.
