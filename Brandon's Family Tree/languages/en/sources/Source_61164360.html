Text
Two area persons were reported in "fair" condition and two others in "good" condition late last night at the Holston Valley Community Hospital following two separate car wrecks.
Billy Dillow, 20, Route 1, Fall Branch and Patsy Simpson, 19, Route 2, Church Hill, were listed as "fair" at the hospital.
Dillow lost control of his 1950 car in which Miss Simpson was riding and went over an embankment on Mill Creek Road at 7:45 p.m., according to Trooper Lloyd Coates.
Coats said Dillow's car plunged 60 feet off the road and turned over once. The car was a total loss, he said.
Ruth Ferguson, 22, 907 Riverside and Charles Bishop, 17, 1737 Buckles Dr. were in "good" condition.
Bishop was driving a 1946 auto in which Miss Ferguson and O. C.
Edens Jr., 19, Church HIll, were riding when Bishop missed a railroad bridge on Carters Valley Road and plunged 50 feet early yesterday, Trooper Jack Sams re-ported.
Bishop suffered shoulder injur-les and Miss Ferguson chest in-Juries. Edens was unhurt.
