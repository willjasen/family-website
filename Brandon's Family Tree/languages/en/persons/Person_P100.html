Notes
On November 25, 1795, Captain John signed a petition to form what became Tazewell County, Virginia. Also, in the Washington County, Virginia "Entries and Survey Book 1" (pages 104 and 113), and the Withe County, Virginia "Deed Book 5" (pages 131, 132, and 236), and "Deed Book 4" (page 387) the records indicate that Captain John Buchanan gave his son Patrick 274 acres on the North Fork of the Holston River on June 21, 1806. Five days later,on June 26, he gave 128 acres in Locust Cove to his son John Buchanan, Jr., and two plots of148 acres each to his son James Buchanan - one plot in Locust Cove and another on the North Fork of the Holston River. [NOTE: He may have anticipated he was near his death and gave the property to his sons just before he died.] NOTE: All research on Captain John Buchanan's property was gathered by Thomas Buchanan Elis and his wife Karen Elis. Correction: I first listed the 1795 date, but he gave property to his sons much later in 1806.
